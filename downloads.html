Downloads

You can download the full game to your PC from here, print it and play it.

The zip file contains the game map in two printable formats (A3 and A4), the rules, the town cards, playing counters and money.

Click on the link to download. Download Here

If you just want to try it out, I would recommend printing it, using felt tip pens to mark the routes and then keeping the map - not for reuse as a map but because it is very entertaining to look at the routes people generated and what scores they get. I found some of these from our games of the 1980s in the box with the rules and it brought back a lot of memories (good and not so good).

If you wanted something more permanent then depending how much kit you have and how much trouble you want to go to, you could laminate the map and mount it on cardboard. The original board from Games Workshop was not great to be honest - unless you wiped it down immediately it always left a mark - so this is not an easy technology. The way forward is obviously to play digitally and I am having a look at that. As they say, watch this space...
